My Notes
Notes

Here's some notes I made for Uni.

In my time at Cambridge, I have often felt that the notes we are given could be improved. So I made some of my own.

The notes are written by and for me, and so they’re quite idiosyncratic, but some people have claimed they’re helpful. Whether that says more about me, those people, or Cambridge I don’t know. For me, colour helps to recognise variables, so you'll often find that the notes are jarringly colourful. Also, I generally try to assume the reader knows most of the relevant maths, with the justification that otherwise the notes would sprawl and I wanted them to be brief, and that they're really supposed to be more about Physics anyway.

  • III Astrophysics
    • Astrophysical Fluid Dynamics. Masterfully taught, but needed a bit more structure, and it was in a weird order imo.
    • Exoplanets. Deceptively in-depth. Looking back, I'm glad it went slowly! Lots of somewhat iffy and qualitative stuff though.
    • Exoplanets (Queloz). I don't have enough evidence to say that winning a Nobel prize makes you a bad lecturer, but I have more than enough to say that being at the Cavendish does.
    • Astrophysical Black Holes. Nice to compress what was a, uh, highly caffeinated lecture course

  • II Astrophysics

      Equations which are given in the Formula Book are in green boxes; equations which aren’t in the FB that I think are important are in red boxes.

    • Principles of Quantum Mechanics. Surprisingly easy to condense. I guess it’s easier to do that when you remove all the extraneous content. Also there’s actual diagrams rather than a faceless guy in a hi-vis.
    • Relativity. Surprisingly hard to condense there’s just so much content in this course. Shamefully long.
    • Stellar Dynamics + Structure of Galaxies. This was very satisfying to make notes for, because ironically the course seemed to have very little structure to it, so it was nice to put things in a decent order.
    • Structure + Evolution of Stars. A lot of the content in the lecture notes is extraneous and has never been examined, so I’ve trimmed a decent amount. Hopefully that makes it easier to wade through.
    • Statistical Physics. Not as short as I would've wanted. Strap in for some long derivations and far too many colours.
    • Cosmology Making these notes made me realise just how all over the place this course was.
    • Astrophysical Fluid Dynamics. Nice as the lecture notes were, there were still places which I thought could be improved.
    • Topics in Astrophysics. Actually not too bad. Some of the derivations are beyond dodgy but oh well...

  • IB Physics
    • Physics A
      • Experimental Methods. Everyone's favourite course...
      • Optics. No oscillations or waves, but at least I tried to actually be consistent with variable names
      • Quantum Physics. The given notes are actually quite good for this, but mine are shorter and more colourful
      • Condensed Matter. Ditto
    • Physics B
      • Electromagnetism. The first set of notes I ever made. The whole point of the colours was originally to help me distinguish B and H.
      • Classical Dynamics. Because some people (myself included) don’t learn well off slides.
      • Thermodynamics. The colours really come into their own here, what with all the thermodynamic variables flying around. I hope your eyes survive.
